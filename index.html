Brent is a User Experience Designer and a graduate from Human Centered Design and Engineering (HCDE) at the University of Washington in Spring 2018. While at the UW, he participated in three Directed Research Groups, interned at a lightweight startup, eVenues.com, he also helped organize MapHacks, a geospatial themed hackathon dedicated to visualizing problems surrounding urban development.

eVenues UX Internship

Summer internship conducting research and competitive analysis. Deliverables included design recommendations, wireframes, and a revised information architecture.

Drone-strike

A Python web app visualizing covert drone strikes carried out by the CIA in Pakistan, Somalia, and Yemen. Hosted on Google App Engine using the Google maps API.

Breez Mobile OS

Design of the user interface and style guide for a mobile OS, including thirteen apps, six system based screens, and an accessibility feature for one-handed use.

Shortcuts for Design

Prototype and usability testing of a shortcut keyboard designed to streamline designers workflows. Built by flashing new USB firmware to an Arduino Uno.

Food Recovery System

Design and research work that focused on building a food recovery app. Tasks included usability testing, a contextual inquiry, storyboarding, and finalizing figma components.

Usability Study

Recruitment and design of a usability study to determine the discoverability, satisfaction, and error correction rate for booking bundled package deals on Expedia.com.
